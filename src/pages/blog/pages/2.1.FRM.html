RFM Model

RFM is a model that is used to segment customers based on their recency, frequency, and monetary value.

- Recency: The time since the customer's last purchase

- Frequency: The number of purchases the customer has made

- Monetary Value: The total amount of money the customer has spent

1. How to use RFM Model

- Calculate the recency, frequency, and monetary value for each customer

- Segment the customers based on the recency, frequency, and monetary value

- Use the segments to target the customers

Recency

Recency is the time since the customer's last purchase. It is a measure of how recently the customer has made a purchase.

Recency is calculated by subtracting the date of the customer's last purchase from the current date.

Frequency

Frequency is the number of purchases the customer has made. It is a measure of how often the customer makes a purchase.

Monetary Value

Monetary Value is the total amount of money the customer has spent. It is a measure of how much the customer has spent.

2. Segmentation

Segmentation is the process of dividing the customers into different groups based on the recency, frequency, and monetary value.

3. Targeting

Targeting is the process of using the segments to target the customers.
